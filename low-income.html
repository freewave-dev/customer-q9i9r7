Low-Income Internet

Unlimited internet starting at $10/month — no contracts, no credit checks, no hidden fees.

If paying for internet is a stretch, there are programs that can get you connected for a fraction of the usual cost. These aren't second-rate connections — they run on the same T-Mobile network as plans that cost 4-5x more. The only difference is the price.

Freewave.Online doesn't sell these plans directly. We point you to the right program, help you figure out if you qualify, and provide support if you need help getting set up.

Option 1: PCS Wireless — $10/month

PCS Low-Cost Internet
$10/mo
Unlimited data on T-Mobile
Typical Home Internet
$50–80/mo
Same network, 5–8x the price

What you get:

  • Unlimited data on T-Mobile's 4G/5G network
  • Hotspot device included (or bring your own)
  • No contracts — cancel anytime
  • No credit check required

Who qualifies:

  • Households receiving government assistance (SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, TANF, WIC, Section 8, free school lunch, Pell Grant, veterans pension, etc.)
  • Household income at or below 200% of the federal poverty guidelines
  • You'll need to verify eligibility during signup

Option 2: Mobile Citizen — $10/month (Nonprofits)

If you run or work at a nonprofit, school, or library, Mobile Citizen offers T-Mobile internet at $10/month for qualifying organizations.

  • Unlimited data on T-Mobile's network
  • Available to 501(c)(3) nonprofits, schools, and libraries
  • Can provide internet to staff, clients, or community members
